Issues Found & Corrected

During our audit, we identified 4 translation issues that could affect the professional appearance of the website. All issues have been corrected.

1. French Spelling Error team.json (French)
Before
"...de bénéficier, elles aussi, d'un soutien stratégique..."
→
After
"...de beneficier, elles aussi, d'un soutien strategique..."
What was wrong: The verb "beneficier" was misspelled as "benefice" - a conjugation error that would be noticeable to French-speaking clients.
2. Financial Terminology Mismatch services.json (English)
Before
"Mezzanine Loans"
→
After
"Participating Loans"
What was wrong: The French term "Prets Participatifs" was incorrectly translated. "Participating Loans" is the accurate English equivalent for this specific French financial instrument, whereas "Mezzanine Loans" refers to a different financing structure.
3. English Text in French Page common.json (French)
Before
"BIRD&. All Rights Reserved."
→
After
"BIRD&. Tous droits reserves."
What was wrong: The copyright notice in the footer was displaying in English even on French pages. This inconsistency could appear unprofessional to French visitors.
4. Missing Dashboard Labels pages.json (Both languages)
Before
Labels missing for dashboard mockups
→
After
FR: "Compte de resultat", "Flux de tresorerie", "KPIs"
EN: "P&L", "Cash Flow", "KPIs"
What was wrong: The financial dashboard mockups displayed on service pages were missing translation keys for their labels. We added proper translations so these elements display correctly in both languages.

Recommendations

Ongoing Translation Maintenance

  • Native speaker review - Consider having a native French speaker review the French content for natural phrasing and any subtle errors we may have missed.
  • New content process - When adding new pages or features, ensure translations are added to both language files simultaneously to maintain parity.
  • Financial term glossary - Maintain a reference document of key financial terms and their approved French/English translations for consistency.
  • Periodic audits - Schedule quarterly reviews of translation files to catch any new content that may have been added without proper translations.
